I'm starting a company that does decommissioning intelligence for offshore wind farms — the first generation of European turbines installed in the early 2010s is hitting end-of-life, and operators are legally required to remove them, but nobody has a defensible model for what a decommissioning project actually costs or how to sequence it. My product is a per-asset decommissioning dossier: I ingest the original installation records, vessel day-rates, scrap-metal forward curves, seabed survey data, and the specific permit conditions for that lease area, then produce a costed, scheduled removal plan an operator can put in front of regulators and insurers. It's me plus one offshore-engineer co-founder, pre-revenue, with two pilot operators who've handed us messy historical project folders to prove we can reconstruct a credible plan. The hard part is that every dossier carries real liability — if our sequencing assumes a heavy-lift vessel can work in a weather window that doesn't exist, or we misread a permit clause about protected seabed, the operator eats millions and we get sued — so I can't just let a model generate plans unsupervised, but I also can't afford to have my co-founder hand-build every dossier or we'll never scale past five clients. I want AI doing the genuine analytical core — the records reconstruction, the cost modeling, the schedule generation, the permit cross-referencing — but I genuinely don't know where the human engineering sign-off has to live, how to structure the work so the AI's reasoning is auditable enough to defend in a dispute, and which parts are too consequential to ever automate. How should I architect this thing from day one?
