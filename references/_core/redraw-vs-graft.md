# Redraw vs graft — the gate every skill opens with

The most common and most fatal error of this era is treating **"X that uses AI"** as **"AI-Native X."**
The difference is not degree, it is **kind**. Every skill in this system opens by applying this gate to
its own domain, because skipping it is how AI-enablement gets relabeled as a rebuild.

## The distinction (generalize the row to your surface)

| | AI-ENABLED (赋能) | AI-NATIVE (原生) |
|---|---|---|
| **Process** | old process + an AI assistant bolted on | the work **redrawn around agents** |
| **Knowledge** | human heads · chat · scattered files | **context flowing through a system** |
| **Human role** | a human drives / approves **every** step | **a few explicit judgment nodes** |

You cannot graft AI onto something architected for the pre-AI era and get a native one — just as you
cannot bolt an electric motor onto a steam-era factory and get a modern factory. **AI speeds up the
nodes; the speed of the whole is set by its graph.** Speeding up anything other than the bottleneck is
an illusion (the documented pattern: ~95% of custom enterprise AI pilots showed no measurable P&L impact
over ~6 months — MIT NANDA).

## The test (apply at the start and again at the end)

> If you deleted the AI from this, would it collapse back into the **old way of doing it** — the same
> roles, steps, and hand-offs? **If yes, you produced AI-enablement, not AI-Native.** Native work has
> agents as the *default* worker and humans/judgment as the *exception* — remove the agents and the work
> doesn't exist in that shape at all.

Per-surface form of the same test:

- **engineering** — delete the agents: does it collapse back to a human typing every line and a manual
  review at every step? Then you sped up coding, you didn't redraw the build.
- **design** — delete the agents: back to one designer hand-crafting one draft? Then AI is a faster
  pencil, not a multi-draft + taste-judgment loop.
- **research** — delete the agents: back to one human reading and synthesizing serially? Then you have a
  faster literature search, not a generation/verification redraw.
- **learning** — delete the agents: back to a human teacher and a textbook? Then AI is a tutor bolted on,
  not a redrawn loop that *protects* the desirable difficulties.
- **innovation** — delete the agents: back to a few people brainstorming? Then you have a faster
  whiteboard, not an abundant-generation + signal-judgment pipeline.
- **org** — delete the agents: back to a normal org chart with the same roles and hand-offs? Then you
  AI-enabled the org, you didn't rebuild it.

## Scope gate — is this even the right thing to do here?

Before doing the work, classify honestly. Refusing the wrong job well is part of the deliverable.

- **Greenfield / from-zero** — there is no legacy to fight. Build generatively from the kernel; the main
  risk is *over-engineering* (a tiny effort does not need the full substrate on day one).
- **Brownfield in place** — you usually **cannot** transform it where it stands; the honest native move
  is to **carve out a new from-zero unit/workstream** and let its output contrast drive change. Editing
  the old graph preserves the topology that causes the bottlenecks.
- **Out of scope (pure AI-enablement)** — "keep the structure, add an AI layer," "make the existing team
  faster." Say so plainly: *this is the adjacent goal, not this methodology.* Then offer the one native
  path (carve out from zero). Don't relabel a copilot rollout as a rebuild.

## The boundary — where AI-Native assists but must not lead

Domains whose core value is **deep human emotional labor** (deep psychological counseling, end-of-life
care, the core relationship of childhood education) and other **strong-trust, high-stakes-judgment**
nodes (clinical decisions, legal liability, safety-critical control): AI-Native can *assist* but must not
*lead*. Design the agent/context layers to support the human at the center; keep agents out of the
relationship's core. Flag this honestly rather than over-claiming a fully agentic design. This is the
kernel respecting its limits, not an exception to it.
