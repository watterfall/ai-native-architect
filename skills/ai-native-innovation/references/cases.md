# Four worked readings — how the compass reads on real cases

Each case pairs one common reading of the compass with a named, real situation. These are
*analytic reconstructions through this skill's frame* — the "why it was bet this way" is the
three-axis reading applied after the fact, not the company's official account. The public facts
(products, acquisitions, usage evolution) are real; the attribution is the analysis. Use them to
calibrate your own readings, not as authority.

## Case 1 · Three-axis triage — why Notion's AI feature deliberately came a step late

A workspace product can ship a generic "AI writing assistant" sidebar in a weekend — the
**viable-path** axis is maxed by the generation side, and the result is a sidebar *identical to
every competitor's* and disconnected from the product's real job. Read on the three axes: viable
path is full (AI inflated it), but **real need** points elsewhere — *in a structured workspace, AI
should help you organize, not write for you.* The later landing — auto-filling database properties,
structuring meeting notes, querying the knowledge base — caught the *original job* rather than
chasing a generated paragraph.

**The load-bearing point is not "Notion bet right."** It is that **"viable path at full" is exactly
the moment to be wary**: when AI maxes that one axis, differentiation can only come from the two axes
it *cannot* help with (real need, inner conviction). Reading all three axes — and flagging the one AI
inflated — is the whole discipline of INSTRUMENT 06.

## Case 2 · Falsification first — an "AI legal assistant" punctured before polish

A common looks-feasible direction: an "AI contract-review assistant" for SMBs. The generation side
writes it impeccably — market size, personas, pricing, tech path, competitor diffs, a ten-page plan
overnight, reading without a seam. This is the *most dangerous* form of looks-feasible: the cosmetics
are perfect, and **the longer you polish it the more it looks true.**

The discipline (INSTRUMENT 12) is to run it through the **falsification point before polish sets in**:
don't ask "where is it good?" — ask "can its falsifying condition be written, and can reality break it
at low cost?" The falsification camp spends *half a day* writing one falsifying sentence and asking
three real users *"how did you get contract review done last time / where were you stuck"* — and
demotes the direction **that day**. The polish camp spends two weeks making a false signal look truer.
Falsifying first nails judgment's anchor before appearance can lift it.

## Case 3 · The useless tree pays back — Slack from the ruins of Tiny Speck's Glitch

A textbook useless-tree story. Stewart Butterfield's company Tiny Speck spent years building a web
game, Glitch, which failed and shut down in 2012 — by any roadmap KPI, a project to be cut clean. The
internal communication tool the team built *for itself* while making the game was, at the time,
worthless byproduct aligned to no KPI. It became Slack.

Had Tiny Speck strictly enforced "align everything to the game KPI, cut anything unrelated," that
tool would never have been grown, let alone recognized after the game failed. **The point is not "keep
everything"** — it is that a team with high useless-tree retention (INSTRUMENT 07) still has, when the
main goal fails, *byproducts efficiency did not cut early*, and those occasionally hide value larger
than the main goal. That most reserves never pay out no more falsifies the reserve's value than most
insurance never paying out falsifies buying it. Redundancy is adaptive reserve, not waste.

## Case 4 · Recognized after the fact — Copilot's "chat" was not planned into being

Code assistants like Copilot were first designed for **inline completion** — you write half a line, it
continues. The turn to **conversation** ("chat") was not planned into the roadmap; it emerged from how
people actually used the tool, was *recognized* within its window, given an instrument, and ratified
into a formal product form.

This is the emergence mark (`habitat-and-emergence.md`): the human's irreplaceability here is **not
because the human can make, but because the human can, in a mass of chaos, recognize the worth-
amplifying anomaly that even the model itself would ignore.** Chat ran exactly the "recognized within
the window" line — had no one read the anomaly in time, it would have been nudged back to "completion"
or cleared as off-pattern usage, and the new species would have died in the filter. The dashboard's two
indicators (recognition latency, amplification hit rate) are precisely what keep that window from being
missed.

---

**What the four readings share:** generate-many is cheap (a weekend sidebar, an overnight plan, a
byproduct tool, an unplanned usage); the scarce act is the *recognition* — reading all three value
axes, falsifying before polish, protecting the byproduct long enough, catching the anomaly in its
window. Direction has no "next step," only — *given this reading now, which way to lean.*
