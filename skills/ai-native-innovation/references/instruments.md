# Instruments — the playable tools the loop runs on

These are the working instruments of the compass. Each is a *reading* you take, not a router that
decides for you — the bet stays human. Use them in loop order: 12 (falsify) → 06 (read worth) →
08 (size the bet); 07 audits the habitat in parallel. Each instrument names what it **feeds** so
the context compounds.

---

## INSTRUMENT 12 — Looks-Feasible Falsifier (use FIRST, before polish)

**Purpose:** "Looks-feasible" is the abundance era's most expensive false signal, because the
generation side can write it up flawlessly — market size, personas, pricing, tech path, competitor
diffs, a ten-page plan overnight, reading without a seam — and *the longer you polish it, the more
real it looks.* The falsifier puts the anchor down **before** polish can lift the appearance.

**How to run it** — hold one concrete direction you are weighing, score three axes:
- **Can you write its falsifying condition?** State what would have to be true for this to be
  *false*. A direction whose falseness you cannot even articulate is unfalsifiable — refuse to bet.
- **Can reality break it cheaply?** Is there a half-day test — write one falsifying sentence, ask
  three real people *"how did you get this done last time / where were you stuck"* — that could
  puncture it today?
- **Is the conviction lived or borrowed?** Does the certainty come from your own friction with the
  world, or from "the AI said so too"?

**The discipline:** don't ask *"where is it good?"* — ask *"can its falseness be written, and can
reality break it at low cost?"* The falsification camp spends half a day and demotes a bad
direction *that day*; the polish camp spends two weeks making a false signal look truer. **Feed:**
the falsification log — the punctured directions and the one sentence that punctured each.

## INSTRUMENT 06 — Value Compass (the three-axis value-perception reading)

**Purpose:** synthesize a "worth" reading on the directions that *survived* falsification. The only
real signal is the **three-way intersection** — abundance in one axis is not the intersection.

**The three axes** (read `../SKILL.md` Step ④ for the full mechanism):
- **Real need** — the job people actually hire a thing to do, told from a patch you have truly
  worked (effectuation's *bird in hand*). AI cannot verify this; it demands leaving the screen.
- **Viable path** — does a path truly exist? The axis AI helps most — but it widens *search*, never
  delivers the *verdict of truth*. **Flag this axis whenever AI inflated it**, because that is the
  exact moment the intersection gets counterfeited.
- **Inner conviction** — lived understanding, not "the AI agreed." Self-test: *if the AI reversed
  itself tomorrow, would my conviction shake?* If yes, it was borrowed.

**Read:** signal is the overlap of all three rings; when AI blows up one ring, that ring's
abundance is *not* the intersection. A direction low on any axis → **do not ask generation to make
it prettier**: cut it, or go earn the missing axis by friction (field test). **Feed:** the
three-axis reading per surviving direction, with the AI-inflated axis flagged, into the portfolio.

## INSTRUMENT 08 — Affordable-Loss × Who-Bears-the-Cost Allocator (size the bet)

**Purpose:** decide *how much* to back, and surface the **other half of "is it worth it?" — who
bears the consequence.** It does not bet for you; it puts the responsibility half on the table.

**Three axes, set one notch each — is this a bet you can *afford, reverse, and answer for*:**
- **Affordable loss** (effectuation) — invest only what you can afford to lose, not what an
  expected-return model predicts (no reliable distribution exists when direction is open).
- **Reversibility** — can a wrong bet be undone? Top-right cell *explore × reversible* (a two-way
  door) is the cell to place **most** of your bets in during the AI era.
- **Consequence-attribution** — on whom does the cost land? This axis is the point of the
  instrument. Most affordable-loss thinking draws only the first axis and reaches the dangerous
  *"it's cheap, no harm trying a lot,"* silently assuming the cost lands only on the one who tries.
  The bottom-right cell — *affordable to you, yet cost spills to others or the future* — is safe on
  axis one and **ought-not** on axis two. The axis turns externality from a forgettable blind spot
  into **a field you must fill in before raising the stake.**

**The rule:** *irreversible × ruinous × cost-offloaded* bets are placed sparingly, slowly, and only
after **pulling the consequence back onto yourself.** Force "who defines value" and "who bears the
cost" onto the **same row** so any decoupling is visible to the naked eye. **Feed:** the bet sheet —
direction → allocation + the one-line who-pays responsibility reading.

This builds an **affordable-loss portfolio**: not a prediction of which bet wins, but downside
control on each, so the *whole portfolio* is something you can afford to lose.

## INSTRUMENT 07 — Useless-Tree Retention Check (audit the habitat, in parallel)

**Purpose:** not a router (it allocates no work) — a mirror of **habitat health.** It checks
whether the useless tree (散木) is being eaten by efficiency. Tick the symptoms of erosion; more
hits = lower retention. Used as an **early warning**: intervening when erosion shows on one or two
symptoms is far cheaper than discovering it once the value source has dried up.

**Symptoms of erosion (the more you tick, the lower retention):**
- All exploration is required to align to a current KPI.
- Slack / slow lanes are cut first whenever capacity tightens (they are unmeasurable).
- The off-main-line exposure surface (where serendipity happens) keeps shrinking.
- Any exploration must re-win a quarterly narrative duel to justify its existence.
- "Learning" is appraised as "output"; byproducts with no immediate KPI get pruned.
- A roadmap drives the metrics-exempt reserve toward zero.

**The fix it points to:** move the useless tree **off the case-by-case justification track** — fence
a metrics-exempt reserve **by institution** (written into the rules) so it is *present by default
unless there is a very strong reason to touch it*, rather than having to re-prove its usefulness
every quarter. The habitat's maintenance cost is almost entirely "guarding the boundary against
erosion by reasonable-sounding reasons." See `habitat-and-emergence.md`. **Feed:** the retention
self-check result into the habitat provisions of the portfolio.
