# Judgment & the stop-line — where the human stays, and why

This is the spine of the execution layer on the innovation surface
(`../../../references/_core/judgment-execution.md`). Generation is abundant; **the bet is the
scarce, value-determining act.** Everything in this file exists to keep that act human and to keep
it a real gate, not a label.

## The allocation rule — who runs each step

A step in the loop goes to an **agent** when *both* are true; otherwise it stays with the **human**:

- **Externalizable** — its output can be written as a spec / signal an AI can read (candidate
  lists, search results, falsifying conditions are all externalizable).
- **Reversible** — if the step's output is wrong, it can be withdrawn at affordable loss.

So: generate, diverge/search, and *draft* falsifying conditions → agents (externalizable +
reversible). Read value-perception, allocate the bet, own the conviction → human (inexternalizable
or irreversible). The two quantities — *externalizability* and *irreversibility of failure* — are
the same two that route work on every surface; here they happen to cut the loop exactly at the bet.

## One-way context flow is a moral architecture, not just a division of labor

Context flows **one way**: real need and inner conviction can be injected **only by the human.**
The agent receives them and can *expand search* on them — but it can never run the arrow backward
and *generate conviction* for the human. This is not a workflow convenience. It is the moral
structure of the surface: the moment value judgment quietly migrates from human to tool, **the
person who pays for the consequence is no longer the person who defined what was worth pursuing**,
and the value-definer / consequence-bearer seam tears. Keeping the arrow one-way is what keeps
those two the same person.

## The stop-line — never offload value-perception

> Generating candidates is abundant; **betting is judgment.** Never offload *which signal is real*
> and *what to back*. Never offload the **conviction** (borrowed conviction is the most dangerous
> false signal — if the AI reversing itself tomorrow would shake it, it was never yours). Never
> offload the **consequence-attribution** (who pays).

Why the line holds and won't be erased by the next model: prediction got cheap, judgment did not.
Selection is capped not by compute but by **the budget a human can be responsible for and stand
behind** (the affordable-loss ceiling) — and that line does not rise as tools cheapen. There is
also a structural-impossibility floor: compressing many people's *heterogeneous* orderings into one
coherent "worth" ranking is impossible under three minimal sanity conditions (Arrow's theorem) — so
the optimizer's only exits are to converge on consensus (grinding heterogeneous value toward the
mean) or to hand the ranking back to a human. The wall is a *theorem*, not an engineering gap.

**The falsification condition (this is a proposition, not a slogan):** if it were shown that
heterogeneous, constitutive value-perception can be *losslessly* systematized — a drill or model
that lets any individual acquire anti-consensus value holding only for another individual, without
degrading to the average — then the stop-line falls and this whole skill should be rewritten.
Current evidence leans the other way (RLCF learns the community average; IndieValueCatalog ~55–65%),
but it is offered on the **exploration ledger** as falsifiable, not asserted as settled law.

## The bet is a real gate with teeth (not a rubber stamp)

A judgment node that is theater is worse than none. The bet gate must:

- **Declare its full verdict set:** `{bet, cut, send-to-field-test}` — and every non-bet verdict
  terminates in a **named** next step, never a silent stall.
  - `bet` → gets an affordable-loss size **and** a one-line who-pays responsibility reading.
  - `cut` → its *reason* is written to the bet-retrospective. Abandonment re-seen from "a loss"
    into "freeing bandwidth for the true signal." A cut with no recorded reason is a leak.
  - `send-to-field-test` → names *what observation would change the verdict* (the missing axis to
    go verify by friction), so it is a routed re-entry, not a parking lot.
- **Refuse to pass on polish.** A "looks-feasible" direction cannot clear the gate by being made
  prettier. Only a *falsified-and-survived* direction with conviction the human owns clears it.
- **Be genuine scrutiny, surfaced by the falsification + retrospective layer** — the human's job at
  the gate is to be *challenged* by the evidence the loop produced, not to wave candidates through.

## Pricing is not judgment — keep the two nodes apart

INSTRUMENT 08's consequence-attribution axis answers *"who pays if it goes wrong."* That is **not**
the same node as *"should this be done at all — is its irreversible harm justified by the value it
creates."* The danger of pricing: once an irreversible harm becomes "a budgetable cost," the
value-definer may start optimizing it like any other expense. "We already bought insurance" must
**not** let the second node be skipped. The third axis keeps that question explicitly on the human
side. Pricing the consequence is allowed; *substituting pricing for judgment* is the failure.
