# Scope & fit — should the compass come out at all?

The compass's most dangerous misuse is **taking it out where direction is not actually open.**
That disguises an execution problem as a value problem and manufactures pointless divergence —
the exact opposite of the skill's purpose. Classify first; refuse the wrong job plainly.

## The master gate, in one line
> **Direction open → use the compass (this skill). Direction locked → use the construction
> drawing (the downstream execution skills).**

## Two gates in series — both must pass

The compass applies only when **both** hold:

1. **Direction is genuinely open.** There are several directions worth weighing, and the question
   is *which is worth it*, not *how to schedule the agreed one into quarters*. If the "what" is
   settled, you have an execution problem — hand it to **ai-native-engineering** (build it) or
   **ai-native-design** (shape its experience). Producing divergence here is theatre.
2. **Failure cost is affordable.** A wrong bet can be withdrawn at a loss you can stand behind
   (affordable loss; effectuation). The explorer's stance — *bet many, bet fast, undo if wrong* —
   depends on this precondition. If one failure cannot be backstopped, the stance is wrong.

## Four situations where the compass explicitly does NOT apply

Saying so flatly is more honest than hedging.

1. **Direction locked (execution scheduling).** The decision is made; what remains is sequencing
   work into quarters. → Downstream construction skills. Don't reopen a settled "what."
2. **Irreversible failure landing on innocent third parties.** Even with several technical
   implementations, if one failure cannot be withdrawn and the cost spills onto people not in the
   room (safety-critical control, a harm with no undo), affordable-loss's precondition fails.
   → Caution closer to **safety engineering**: narrower acceptable bands, more redundancy and
   review — *not* "bet many, bet fast." Flag this; do not apply the risk-tolerant compass stance.
3. **Pure AI-enablement of an existing idea machine.** "Keep our stage-gate funnel / innovation
   lab / hackathon cadence, just add AI to generate more." That is AI-enablement, not a redraw —
   and worse, it pours AI into the generation side, *raising the noise floor.* Say so: the gate
   each legacy structure guards now stands empty (the bottleneck moved to recognition). The native
   move is not to tune the funnel but to swap its goal to *holding the habitat of value
   perception.* See `false-signals.md` for the legacy-structure diagnosis.
4. **Deep human emotional-labor / strong-trust core.** Where the relationship *is* the value
   (deep counseling, end-of-life care, the core of childhood education) or where conviction and
   consequence are inseparable from a specific human's stake. → **Boundary.** AI may assist
   generation and search, but must not author the conviction or own the consequence. Keep the
   human at the value bedrock; flag honestly, do not over-claim a fully agentic design. This is the
   kernel respecting its limits (`../../../references/_core/redraw-vs-graft.md`), not an exception.

## The redraw-vs-graft illusion at this boundary

The most common illusion is *"AI makes everything redoable, so every direction is open."* It is
not. Cheaper tools expand the **adjacent possible** — the ring of places you can reach in one
step grows a hundredfold — but they do **not** create value from nothing: the number of places
*worth* going barely grows. So "everything is redoable" is precisely the abundance that collapses
signal-to-noise. Expanded reach is a gift *and* a curse; treating it as pure license to diverge is
how you drown the few real signals.

## The verbatim out-of-scope redirect (use this language)

> "Your direction is already settled — the real question here is *execution*, not *worth*. This is
> the adjacent goal, not this methodology. Running a value compass on it would manufacture
> divergence you don't need and disguise a build/schedule problem as a value problem. Take it to
> the construction layer (engineering / design). I'll apply the compass the moment the *what* is
> genuinely open again."

Refusing well is part of the deliverable. Do not relabel an execution problem as innovation to
look busy — that is the micro-form of innovation theatre.
