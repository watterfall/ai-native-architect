# Habitat & emergence — the half you cannot drill

Value-perception splits along an **externalizability gradient.** The part near *settled community
consensus* can be written, labelled, and trained as a reward signal — that half gets **drills**
(see `instruments.md` and the loop in `../SKILL.md`). The part near the *anti-consensus frontier* is
a conviction earned only by an individual's long friction with the world, unexpressible as a rule;
forcing it into a system merely grinds it toward the mean. That half cannot be taught — it can only
have its **emergence conditions cultivated.** This file is the ecology branch: build it a habitat.

## The systematization fork — why "both tracks" is not fence-sitting

The fork is forced by a theorem, not a compromise. Consensus is learnable: RLCF (reinforcement
learning from community feedback) is exactly the externalization of the consensus end — but the same
evidence shows **over-optimization crowds out the anti-consensus**, i.e. what is learnable is exactly
the *homogenizing* part. Arrow's impossibility theorem says no aggregation can compress heterogeneous
orderings into one coherent "worth" ranking without collapsing to dictatorship — so the optimizer's
only exits are *converge on consensus* (grind heterogeneity toward the mean) or *hand the ranking back
to a human*. Therefore: hand the consensus stretch to the **training manual** (fold it into abundance,
no need to pretend it's mysterious); hand the heterogeneous stretch to the **ecology guide** (sink it
into the value bedrock, no need to pretend it's teachable). The criterion is sharp, not fuzzy: **can
the piece in hand be written as a bookkeepable trace?** Yes → training manual. No → habitat.

**Which way to bet when the boundary is uncertain — ecology as the floor.** If you wrongly *train* a
piece that was actually constitutive, you systematically manufacture the average, and because the
output "looks like innovating" (innovation theatre) the error is hard to self-detect — an
**irreversible** cost. If you wrongly *keep* space that could in fact have been trained, the worst
case is "kept a bit more room than needed" — **reversible and bearable.** Betting the weight toward
the side you can retreat from is itself a demonstration of this skill's value judgment: not gambling
on which side is right, but controlling the downside of being wrong. (Note the boundary is not fixed:
the automation front moves right over time as more judgment becomes externalizable — but the
anti-consensus right end has a floor. Whether that floor can be breached is the decisive frontier
open question; treat it as falsifiable, on the exploration ledger.)

## The useless-tree reserve (散木) — negative engineering against efficiency's gravity

A habitat works mainly by **subtraction.** The default gravity is efficiency, and it eats redundancy:

- **Freed capacity does not become slack on its own.** Capacity saved by a tool is reallocated to
  *more of the same* (more volume), not to something different — the efficiency paradox ("polishing a
  better steam engine while the world turns to electricity"). And "what cannot be measured gets cut,"
  so slack — being unmeasurable — is always cut first. Every AI deployment emits a "progress" signal
  (faster, cheaper, more output) and those signals land almost entirely on the *exploitation* side;
  AI amplifies exploitation, not exploration.
- **"Optimal ≠ leanest" is not a moral plea — it has hard evolutionary-biology evidence:** neutral
  networks and gene duplication show redundancy is *adaptive reserve*, not waste. So protecting the
  useless tree is an **engineering requirement** against the system's default, not an exhortation.
- **Serendipity is a designable exposure surface, not luck.** It needs two conditions at once: a
  *prepared mind* (able to recognize that what it stumbled on has value) and an *exposure surface*
  large enough and off the main line (so stumbling can happen). The AI era systematically compresses
  the second — when all exploration aligns to the measurable goal, the off-main-line surface vanishes
  and the serendipity hit rate goes to zero. The reserve turns that surface from *what luck leaves
  over* into *what is deliberately fenced*.
- **Fence it by institution, with a hard boundary.** A metrics-exempt reserve must be *written into
  the rules* so it need not re-win a narrative duel every quarter. The hard boundary's function is not
  physical isolation but turning the useless tree from "having to win an argument every time" into
  "present by default unless there is a very strong reason to touch it." Audit with INSTRUMENT 07.

## Diversity as insurance against the pull to the mean

Diversity is not political correctness — it is **insurance against the gravity that pulls everything
toward the average.** The enemy of heterogeneity is *over-optimization of a single objective*, not the
machine itself (drop the single objective — novelty-search, MAP-Elites, open-ended algorithms — and
machines produce heterogeneity too). The habitat's job is to keep different values each surviving
*without being flattened*: independent exploration units, appraisal by *learning* rather than *output*,
explicit defense of time that sits on no KPI.

## The emergence dashboard — recognize the new species in its window

Emergence cannot be **produced**, only **recognized after the fact** (事后认出新物种). The leverage
point of innovation climbs floor by floor: **make → scaffold → read.** The new scarce act is not
producing one more candidate but *recognizing the one already grown that is worth amplifying.*

- **The explanation/translation layer is the bottleneck.** As output races past what humans can
  digest, you cannot recognize a new species you cannot read at all. But a *naive* auto-summary is
  worse than none here: it drops exactly the anomalies that are most counter-intuitive and least
  legible — the part most likely to *be* a new species — pressing them back toward the mean (the
  conservative bias). A real explanation layer preserves the signals that "look off, but might be
  something new" instead of clearing them as noise.
- **The recognition window.** An anomalous usage forks into two fates: within the window — grown
  visible enough, not yet cleared as noise/abuse — someone watching asks *"is this a real need I did
  not design for?"* and it rises into a new species, given an instrument and ratified into a formal
  form. Miss the window and it is nudged back on track or cleared as an anomaly, and the new species
  dies in the filter. (Copilot Chat ran the "recognized within the window" line — see `cases.md`.)
- **Two leading indicators.** The dashboard exists to keep the window from being missed — not to
  produce emergence but to ensure that *when* it happens someone can see it and still has time to
  ratify it. Track **recognition latency** (how long from anomaly-appearing to someone-noticing) and
  **amplification hit rate** (of the anomalies amplified, how many paid off). The hard part is the
  **amplification decision** — acting while it is still unclear, against the conservative bias that
  presses anomalies back to the mean. **Feed:** the emergence log — anomalies seen, ratified-or-
  cleared, and how fast.
