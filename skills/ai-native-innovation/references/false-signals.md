# False-signal guards — the six ways it goes wrong, and how to catch them

"Looks-feasible" is the abundance era's most expensive false signal: generation writes it
flawlessly, and polishing makes it look *truer*, not *more real*. This file is the guard layer —
six failure patterns with leading indicators and fixes, three system-level failures, and the
recalibration that demotes "it sounds right" from evidence down to candidate.

The root shared by all of them: **amid abundance, doubt appearance by default and deliberately hunt
for the falsifying condition.** Once that habit is internalized, it *is* value-perception in this
skill's sense.

## The six ways it goes wrong (leading indicator · fix)

1. **Borrowed conviction (借来的确信).** Conviction sourced from "the AI also said so," not from your
   own friction with the world. *Indicator:* the "if the AI reversed itself tomorrow" test shakes
   the bet. *Fix:* go get friction first — leave the screen and verify the real need before betting.
2. **The looks-feasible trap.** A direction polished to perfection that rides only the viable-path
   axis. *Indicator:* high feasibility-of-plan, low or unverified real-need. *Fix:* falsify before
   polish (INSTRUMENT 12); cut it if it cannot be falsified or fails the real-need axis.
3. **Unfalsifiable direction.** You cannot even write the condition under which it would be false.
   *Indicator:* every objection gets absorbed into the story. *Fix:* refuse to bet until a
   falsifying condition can be stated.
4. **Mistaking volume for progress (the safety of looking busy).** Keeping ten candidate directions
   feels more diligent and safer than betting on two. *Indicator:* the portfolio grows but the bet
   count and abandon count don't move. *Fix:* generate many, **bet few and sharp**; the real mark of
   improving signal-to-noise is *fewer, sharper bets and harder cuts*, not more candidates.
5. **Efficiency eats redundancy.** All exploration is forced to align to current KPIs and the useless
   tree's retention trends to zero. *Indicator:* INSTRUMENT 07 lights up. *Fix:* fence a
   metrics-exempt reserve by institution (`habitat-and-emergence.md`).
6. **Externality-blindness.** The cost lands on people not in the room. *Indicator:* the bet looks
   clean only because the objective function never mentioned the spillover. *Fix:* run every bet
   through INSTRUMENT 08's consequence-attribution axis — *on whom does the cost land?*

## Three system-level failures (the org-scale forms)

- **Innovation theatre.** An org falls in love with activities that *look like innovating* —
  hackathons, innovation labs, the count of AI pilots — and their output is precisely the most
  easily-generated "looks-feasible." *The test is simple:* "how many of these bets actually staked an
  affordable loss and actually went to verify a real need?" If the answer is near zero, it is
  theatre. (Note the cargo-cult "fail fast": the original had an omitted precondition — each failure
  must be *cheap* and must *teach something*. Copying "fail more" while dropping "affordable +
  learnable" makes failure neither cheap nor instructive — and under generation-abundance, *quickly
  trying many looks-feasible directions is the most expensive failure of all.*)
- **Externality-blindness.** A more hidden form where even the harmed someone is not in the room. AI
  is the *amplifier*, not the cause: it optimizes the objective function you give it, is blind to any
  externality not written into that function, and writes the plan ever cleaner — making "the cost not
  seen" vanish from the appearance entirely. *Fix:* the consequence-attribution axis forces the cost
  to be stated, refusing to let "the objective function didn't mention it" pretend it doesn't exist.
- **Optimizing the measurable to death.** Goodhart's law in innovation form: once a proxy metric
  (active users, idea count, patent count) is taken *for* innovation, the system optimizes the proxy
  and squeezes out the real, hard-to-measure value. *Fix:* ask of any metric — *is this squeezing out
  the real value?*

## Why the legacy structures can't catch any of this

The twentieth-century innovation machine — stage-gate funnel, KPI roadmap, hackathon, idea-count
metric, "fail fast," central R&D lab — all assumed **credible plans are scarce**, so the gate at each
stage passes on *appearance-maturity* (Cooper's Stage-Gate). But appearance is exactly what the
generation side now supplies without limit, so the funnel today filters for *who is best at making a
plan read polished*, systematically passing the high-feasibility / low-real-need looks-feasible trap
— the form most able to clear a gate. Move the bottleneck to recognition and **the gate each
structure guards stands empty.** They don't need tuning; their premise is gone. The fix is not "repair
the funnel" but to swap the machine's goal from *efficiently advance the idea flow* to *faithfully
hold the habitat of value perception*.

## How to recalibrate — demote "it sounds right" from evidence to candidate

The single recalibration that powers all the fixes: treat *coherence* (it reads convincingly) as a
**candidate**, never as **evidence**. A plausible plan earns the right to be *falsified*, not the
right to be *funded*. Evidence is what survives a cheap reality-test; the only proxy that did not
depreciate as tools cheapened is *"can a falsifying condition be constructed, and can reality break
it."* Keep two ledgers separate: a **reliability ledger** for established claims, and an
**exploration ledger** for leading indicators (hit rate, abandon rate, useless-tree retention,
recognition latency, amplification hit rate) that a team must keep over time to calibrate — offered
as falsifiable signals, not asserted as proven reality.
