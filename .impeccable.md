## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are founders, operators, and team leads evaluating whether the AI-Native methodology applies
to their organization. They arrive with practical pressure: deciding whether to redesign a venture, carve
out a new unit, install the seven skills, or continue with ordinary AI enablement. They need a fast,
credible entry point that clarifies the structural question before sending them into the full methodology.

### Brand Personality
Precise, serious, and diagrammatic. The interface should feel like an editorial atlas or methodology
frontispiece: spare, confident, and intellectually direct, but not sensational or adversarial.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a restrained bilingual editorial-map style. Favor a strong cover-like first screen, high contrast,
calm typography, exact diagrams, and a small number of purposeful interactions. Avoid generic AI startup
visuals, neon gradients, dark cyberpunk, card-heavy SaaS layouts, and headline-copy that feels like clickbait.

### Design Principles
- Lead with the structural premise: when execution is no longer scarce, the organization must be redesigned.
- Keep the buffer page minimal: one core proposition, one countdown, three actions.
- Let the full methodology remain the canonical destination; this repo's page is an entry and cache layer.
- Use visuals to clarify execution surfaces, not to decorate them.
- Preserve bilingual parity for public-facing copy: Chinese and English should each read naturally.
